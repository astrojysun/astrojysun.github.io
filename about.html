About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Astronomy at the Ohio State University (advisor: Adam Leroy). I am also an active member of the PHANGS collaboration, and a large part of my research is within the scope of this collaboration. Before joining OSU, I received a B.Sc. in Astrophysics at Tsinghua University in 2015.

As an astrophysist, I would like to understand the physics that shapes the interstellar medium and regulates star formation in nearby galaxies. While I specialize in analyzing millimeter and radio interferometric data, my research has used a wide variety of multiwavelength observations as well as "synthetic observations" in numerical simulations. More details to follow on my Research page!

In my leisure time, I love listening to classic music and playing piano. Beethoven, Schubert, Brahms, and Mahler are among my favorite composers. I used to attend live concerts at Ohio Theatre and Severance Hall frequently, but the 2020 pandemic has forced me to rely more on Spotify. I also enjoy reading books on many topics and recently have developed an interest in philosophy of science. I might characterize my current self as more of an instrumentalist than a realist, but I very much look forward to exploring new territories and modifying my web of belief accordingly.
